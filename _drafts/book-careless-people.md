## Description

*Careless People* is a memoir by Sarah Wynn-Williams, a former Facebook (Meta) global policy director who worked at the company from 2011 to 2017. The book offers an insider account of the social media giant's rise to global dominance, detailing her experiences navigating the company's culture, its relationships with world leaders, and the internal tensions as Facebook's influence — and the awareness of its harms — grew.

Wynn-Williams writes candidly about the idealism that drew her and others to Facebook's mission of "connecting the world," and how that idealism was gradually eroded by corporate priorities. The title is a nod to F. Scott Fitzgerald's phrase in *The Great Gatsby* about "careless people" who "smashed up things and creatures and then retreated back into their money."

*Note: After publication, Meta sought and obtained a temporary gag order against Wynn-Williams, which generated significant press attention. The book quickly became a bestseller.*
