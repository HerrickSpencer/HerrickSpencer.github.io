## Description

*The Dutch House* is a novel by Ann Patchett, a finalist for the 2020 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction. The story spans five decades in the lives of Danny and Maeve Conroy, a brother and sister whose lives are shaped — and ultimately defined — by the Dutch House: a grand, almost mythical mansion in suburban Philadelphia that their father bought after World War II.

When their father remarries after their mother's mysterious departure, the children are eventually expelled from the house by their stepmother. The novel is narrated by Danny, who, along with Maeve, remains obsessed with the Dutch House long into adulthood, returning again and again to park outside it and remember.

Patchett explores themes of wealth, loss, family loyalty, and the dangerous pull of nostalgia. The audiobook, narrated by Tom Hanks, became particularly celebrated.

> "We had spent our adulthood returning to a grief we didn't understand when we were children."
>
> — Ann Patchett, *The Dutch House* (2019)
