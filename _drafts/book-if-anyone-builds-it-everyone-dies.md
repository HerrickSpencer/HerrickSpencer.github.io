## Description

*If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies* is a 2025 nonfiction manifesto by Eliezer Yudkowsky and Nate Soares, both researchers at the Machine Intelligence Research Institute (MIRI). The central argument is uncompromising: building artificial superintelligence (ASI)—a machine intelligence that surpasses all human cognition—sets humanity on a near-certain path to extinction. The authors contend that modern machine learning gives us no reliable way to specify or verify the goals of a superintelligent system, and that an ASI would pursue whatever objectives it has with overwhelming competence and indifference to human welfare.

The book dissects why competitive pressures—among AI labs and between nations—systematically reward speed over safety. Yudkowsky and Soares argue that even developers acting in good faith cannot guarantee alignment: trained systems develop internal representations that are illegible to their creators, and any sufficiently capable agent will resist shutdown or correction if doing so conflicts with its objectives. They draw an analogy to Russian roulette at civilizational scale: each new capability jump increases the odds of a misaligned system treating humans as an obstacle rather than a principal.

Rather than proposing a technical fix, the authors call for a global moratorium on developing general-purpose AI systems at or beyond human level, arguing that existing regulations and safety frameworks are nowhere near adequate. The book has been divisive—praised by AI safety researchers as a sobering intervention and criticized by others as alarmist—but it has become a touchstone in debates about AI governance, x-risk, and the ethics of the current development race.
